# Day Planner

**GitHub:** https://github.com/lynchjames/obsidian-day-planner

## What is the Day Planner Plugin?

**Day Planner** transforms Obsidian into a **time-blocked planning system** with visual daily schedules. It lets you:

- 📅 Create time-based schedules for your day
- ⏰ Block out specific times for tasks and meetings
- 👀 See your entire day at a glance
- 🎯 Stay on track with visual time management
- 📊 Drag-and-drop rescheduling
- 🔔 Get reminders for upcoming tasks
- 🔗 Link scheduled tasks to your notes
- 📈 Track time spent on activities

## Why Use Day Planner?

**Day Planner brings structure and visibility to your daily schedule:**

- **See the full picture** — Visual timeline shows your entire day
- **Better time management** — Allocate realistic time blocks
- **Reduce context-switching** — Know what you should be doing each hour
- **Avoid overcommitment** — See when your day is actually full
- **Stay focused** — One task at a time, clearly defined
- **Track time usage** — Understand where your time actually goes
- **Reduce stress** — Clear schedule reduces decision fatigue
- **Improve productivity** — Time-blocking increases deep work time

## Key Features at a Glance

| Feature | What It Does |
|---------|------------|
| **Time-blocked View** | See your day as hourly blocks with tasks |
| **Drag & Drop** | Reschedule tasks by dragging to new time slots |
| **Task Duration** | Set how long each task takes |
| **Recurring Tasks** | Schedule repeating tasks automatically |
| **Time Reminders** | Get alerts when it's time for a task |
| **Note Integration** | Link tasks to your detailed notes |
| **Color Coding** | Categorize tasks by color |

## How Day Planner Works

1. **Create time blocks** — Define tasks with specific times
2. **Visualize your day** — See all tasks on an hourly timeline
3. **Adjust as needed** — Drag tasks to new times
4. **Execute** — Follow the schedule throughout the day
5. **Review** — Reflect on what you accomplished

## Tips for Effective Time Blocking

💡 **Realistic estimates** — Add 20% buffer; plans always take longer
💡 **Protect deep work** — Schedule focus time before meetings fill the day
💡 **Build in breaks** — Every 90 minutes of focus needs recovery
💡 **Group similar tasks** — Batching reduces context-switching cost
💡 **Don't schedule back-to-back** — Leave transition time
💡 **Review and adjust** — Track actual time and refine estimates
💡 **Respect your rhythms** — Schedule work when you're naturally productive
💡 **Protect personal time** — Schedule self-care as seriously as work

## Overcoming Planning Challenges

**Challenge: Tasks always run over**
→ Add 25% time padding to estimates
→ Schedule fewer things than you think possible

**Challenge: Meetings derail the day**
→ Schedule all meetings in one block (if possible)
→ Protect morning for deep work
→ Use calendar rules to prevent back-to-back meetings

**Challenge: Plans feel rigid**
→ Use blocks as guidelines, not absolute rules
→ Build in buffer time for flexibility
→ Review and adjust weekly
→ Distinguish "must-do" from "want-to-do"

**Challenge: Can't focus on one thing**
→ Use visible time blocks to stay focused
→ Set phone reminders for block transitions
→ Close distracting applications
→ Use Pomodoro: 25 min focus + 5 min break
