# WeRead

**GitHub:** https://github.com/zhaohongxuan/obsidian-weread-plugin

## What is the WeRead Plugin?

**WeRead Plugin** syncs your **WeRead highlights and notes** directly into Obsidian, transforming your reading into a knowledge management system. It lets you:

- 📚 Import books from your WeRead library automatically
- 🎯 Capture highlights and annotations without manual work
- 📝 Organize reading notes by book and topic
- 🔗 Link book insights to your existing notes
- 📊 Create a reading database you can query and analyze
- 🗂️ Build a personal library with metadata (author, rating, progress)
- 🔍 Search and reference your reading across your vault

## Why Use the WeRead Plugin?

**WeRead Plugin keeps your reading knowledge where it belongs—in your vault:**

- **No manual copying** — Highlights sync automatically from WeRead
- **Centralized knowledge** — Reading notes live with your other research
- **Better retention** — Reviewing highlights in Obsidian aids learning
- **Connected insights** — Link book ideas to related projects and notes
- **Reading database** — Search highlights across all your books
- **Context preserved** — Keep chapter and page information for reference
- **Workflow integration** — Review readings during note-taking

## How the WeRead Plugin Works

1. **Connect your account** — Link your WeRead account to Obsidian
2. **Sync your library** — Import books from your WeRead collection
3. **Fetch highlights** — Download all your highlights and notes
4. **Auto-create notes** — Plugin generates notes for each book with highlights
5. **Organize and link** — Connect reading insights to your research and projects

## Syncing Strategy

**Recommended setup:**
1. **Weekly sync** — Set up scheduled syncs to keep notes current
2. **Active books** — Focus on books you're currently reading
3. **Reviewed highlights** — Occasionally revisit old highlights
4. **Archive completed** — Move finished books to archive section
5. **Backup originals** — Keep highlight links back to WeRead for reference
