# How to Identify Your Limiting Beliefs
## 1. Structured Excavation
*A limiting belief always hides behind a strong feeling. Work backwards from emotion to belief.*
### On capability
- What am I secretly afraid people will find out about me?
- What achievements do I have that I still don't fully believe I deserve?
- When do I feel like a fraud?
### On worthiness:
- What kind of success feels too big for someone like me?
- What would X say if they knew my real ambitions?
- What do I want but haven't let myself want out loud?
### On risk:
- What's the worst I'm actually afraid will happen if I fail publicly?
- What does failure mean about me as a person?
- What have I been "getting ready" for longer than 3 months?
### On relationships:
- What do I believe I need to be/do to be loved?
- What kind of person do I believe I'll end up with, honestly?
- What do I believe about my own lovability?

## 2. Real-time Capture
*Train yourself to catch those specific signals about limiting beliefs*
### Language signals — watch for these words in your own head:
- _"I'm not ready yet"_
- _"Someone else would do this better"_
- _"What if they think I'm..."_
- _"I should probably wait until..."_
- _"I don't want to seem..."_

### Behaviour signals: 
- Hesitating before sending something --- why?
- Reopening a finished piece to edit again --- why?
- Talking yourself out of reaching out to someone --- why?
- Feeling relief when an opportunity doesn't work out --- why?

```
Trigger: [what happened]
Feeling: [what I felt]
Belief: [what I must believe for this reaction to make sense]
Source: mine or hers?
```

## 3. The Belief Checks
_A direct list to react to — check which ones land._

Read each one and notice your body's response — not your rational mind:
- I'm not experienced enough to be taken seriously
- If I fail publicly it will define me
- I need to be perfect before I can be visible
- People like me don't achieve things like that
- If I'm too successful I'll become a target
- I don't fully deserve what I have
- Being ambitious is arrogant
- If I ask for help I look weak
- I'm behind where I should be at my age
- Good things don't last
- I have to earn love and approval through performance
- Uncertainty means danger

**Mark each one:** strongly resonates / partially / doesn't land

Once you have your list, for each belief run Byron Katie's four questions:
1. Is it true?
2. Can I absolutely know it's true?
3. How do I react when I believe this thought?
4. Who would I be without this thought?
